About the Project

This is a 3D digitization of the flip poem Cold Mountain (1967) by Canadian poet bpNichol. The original book was released as part of the collection JOURNEYING & the returns published by Coach House Press and is now held in the bp Nichol Fonds at Simon Fraser University's Rare Books and Special Collections. As a handmade concrete poem, Cold Mountain has unique material and sculptural qualities that challenge standard textual digitization practices. This project was therefore conceived as a way to work with such divergent formal aspects to theorize a speculative model of digitization that allows for greater degrees of interactivity. The 3D .blend file hosted here was modelled by SFU English MA student Donald Shipton using a rigged book asset designed by Liu Ran. To interact with and explore this book yourself, download the .blend file hosted here and open it in the open-source 3D modelling software, Blender.

About Me

Donald Shipton is an MA student in the Department of English at Simon Fraser University. His scholarly interests reside primarily in contemporary Canadian literature and the archive.
